Accommodation

We'll be staying in Limassol, Alex's hometown. It's a 30 minute drive from the venue, but we will organize bus transportation from Limassol to the venue.

Hotels in Limassol are plentiful and generally good, but here are some suggestions:

Limassol Tourist Area

Most hotels are on a seafront strip to the east of the city center. The beaches are nicer, but getting to the old town will require a car, bus or bike ride.

You can find a bunch of places on Booking.com; some suggestions include:

Crowne Plaza - 4 stars 180 EUR/night
Located in between the Tourist Area to the east and the old part of Limassol. A bit more of a business hotel; still has beach and pool but smaller and possibly fewer children running around than the above options. We have a group rate here for 170-210 EUR/night depending on length of stay and room. Click for more details! Bookings must be made before the end of June.

Four Seasons - 5 stars ~400 EUR/night
Amathus - 5 stars ~300 EUR/night
Generally regarded as the ‚Äòbest‚Äô hotels in Limassol, with prices to match. Both have beach access, great restaurants, and multiple pools.

Mediterranean Hotel - 4 stars ~200 EUR/night
Located between the hotels mentioned above. Similar pool and beach situation, but a not at the same level of food and service.

Hotel Apartments 50-200 EUR/night
There's a good selection of hotel apartments in the tourist area at a variety of price ranges. Things to check include proximity to the beach and to more built up areas.

Limassol Old Town

Sir Paul - ~150 EUR/night (single occupancy from 105 EUR)
New boutique hotel in a lovely old building with high ceilings, exposed rock walls, and a courtyard. No pool and not on the beach, but easily walkable to anywhere in old town and the seafront. Limited parking.

We have booked a block of rooms here at corporate rates. Please email sirpaul@sirpaulhotel.com and mention "Kathryn Wheeler and Alex Toumazis's Wedding" to book with these rates.

Curium Palace - ~150 EUR/night
The oldest hotel in Limassol. Has a pool but is relatively small and boutiquey. It's a 5 minute walk from the beach, or a 20+ minute walk along the beach to the heart of old town.

Outside Limassol

There are a range of Airbnb options in nearby, picturesque villages. These generally are in foothills relatively far from the sea. Depending on the location you may need to find your own transportation to and from the wedding venue (in Delikipos), but we can assist with booking taxis. This can be a very affordable option, as they're not as in-demand due to their distance from the sea.

The best time to book a flight is always a mystery, but we'd suggest booking flights at least 3 months out. Google lets you track the prices of flights, which is useful if you want to plan something out and then wait to book it.

Flights (from the U.K.)

BA, Easyjet, Wizz Air, Ryanair and more all fly direct from various London airports (and some other U.K. airports). You can fly to either Larnaca (LCA) or Paphos (PFO) -- they're about equidistant from Limassol. Here's an example search for flights from London to Cyprus from Thursday to Monday; as of December, the cheapest option was EasyJet at ¬£121, but there are options on BA and several other airlines under ¬£200.

For other European cities, Google flight search can be a useful resource. Many large cities will have direct flights by national airlines, and several budget airlines fly routes to Cyprus.

Flights (from the U.S.)

There are no direct flights from the U.S. to Cyprus. When booking flights, you can either try and book the complete trip as a single booking, or book a flight to somewhere and then a separate booking to Cyprus. The latter can often be cheaper, especially if you would like to spend some time somewhere else in Europe. Note that due to time zones and flight time, you will arrive in at least one day (and sometimes two) after you leave the U.S.

For just Cyprus, Google can be pretty easy. Here's an example search for flights from New York to Cyprus from Tuesday to Monday; as of December, the cheapest option is Lufthansa for $744, but there are also reasonable Aeroflot, BA, and Norweigian+Easyjet options. Here's an example search for flights from the Bay Area to Cyprus for the same dates; this is a bit more limited but there are cheap options with Norwegian/Easyjet.

If you want to look at multiple bookings, take a look at the above section for flights from Europe, and then match up one of them with a flight from the U.S. For example, you could book a SF to Rome return trip for around $400 and then spend a night or two in Italy and book a Rome to Cyprus return for around $250. Note that this ends up significantly cheaper than a single booking! Google's map that shows up when you haven't entered a destination can be useful for finding cities to stop over in.

Alex has extensive experience booking flights to Cyprus, so feel free to reach out with specific questions!
